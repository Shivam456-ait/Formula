Class 10 Chemistry Important Formulas

🧪 Mole Concept

Moles (n) = Given Mass / Molar Mass

Number of Particles = Moles × 6.022 × 10²³

Molar Mass = Sum of Atomic Masses

Mass = Moles × Molar Mass

Moles = Number of Particles / 6.022 × 10²³

⚗️ Concentration & Solutions

C₁V₁ = C₂V₂

Mass % = (Mass of Solute / Mass of Solution) × 100

Volume % = (Volume of Solute / Volume of Solution) × 100

Molarity (M) = Moles of Solute / Volume of Solution (in Litres)

Normality (N) = Gram Equivalent / Volume (in Litres)

Strength = Molarity × Molar Mass

🔥 Chemical Reactions

Percentage Yield = (Actual Yield / Theoretical Yield) × 100

Rate of Reaction = Change in Concentration / Time

⚡ Acids, Bases & Salts

pH = -log[H⁺]

pOH = -log[OH⁻]

pH + pOH = 14

🔬 Atomic Structure

Number of Protons = Atomic Number

Number of Electrons = Atomic Number

Number of Neutrons = Mass Number - Atomic Number

Valency = Electrons Lost or Gained

🌡️ Gas Laws (Basic)

Boyle’s Law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

Charles Law: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

Combined Gas Law: P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂
