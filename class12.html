Electrochemistry

E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode

ΔG° = −nFE°cell

Ecell = E°cell − (0.0591/n) log Q

Faraday Constant (F) = 96500 C/mol

Conductivity (κ) = G × (l/A)

Molar Conductivity (Λm) = κ × 1000 / C

Chemical Kinetics

Rate = k[A]^m[B]^n

First Order: t₁/₂ = 0.693 / k

Second Order: t₁/₂ = 1 / k[A₀]

Arrhenius Equation: k = Ae^(-Ea/RT)

ln k = ln A − Ea/RT

Activation Energy: Ea = 2.303R (log k₂/k₁) × (T₁T₂ / (T₂−T₁))

Solutions

Molarity (M) = Moles / Volume (L)

Molality (m) = Moles / Mass of Solvent (kg)

Normality (N) = Gram Equivalent / Volume (L)

ΔTb = Kb × m

ΔTf = Kf × m

Osmotic Pressure (π) = CRT

Van’t Hoff Factor (i) = Observed ΔTf / Normal ΔTf

Surface Chemistry

Freundlich Isotherm: x/m = kP^(1/n)

Langmuir Isotherm: θ = KP / (1 + KP)

d & f Block Elements

Magnetic Moment (μ) = √(n(n+2)) BM

n = number of unpaired electrons

Coordination Compounds

Effective Atomic Number (EAN) = Z − Oxidation State + 2 × No. of Ligands

Crystal Field Stabilization Energy (CFSE) formulas

Organic Chemistry

Alcohol Oxidation: R−OH → R−CHO → R−COOH

Carboxylic Acid: R−COOH

Amine Basicity depends on electron donating groups

Rate of SN1 ∝ [Alkyl Halide]

Rate of SN2 ∝ [Alkyl Halide][Nucleophile]
